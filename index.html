About Me

My foundation lies in core statistics — probability, inference, linear models, regression, and time series — but I’m happiest when these tools meet real-world data.

I’ve worked on projects ranging from predicting insurance uptake and modelling Parkinson’s disease progression to analyzing time series patterns and classifying comments from online credit card discussions. These problems blend human behaviour with data, something I find fascinating.

Alongside academics, I’ve contributed to student life as a Student Companion (ISCP), mentoring juniors, and as an Interview Coordinator (IPT), helping organize placement processes. I also enjoy photography — winning first place in the “Campus Kaleidoscope” competition — as well as table tennis, chess, carrom, and swimming.

Insurance Uptake Classification

understanding customer behaviour - Classification

Built a classification model to predict customers who would be interested in buying insurance. My work included data cleaning, feature engineering, comparing different models, evaluating metrics for imbalanced data, and interpreting model insights to support more focused outreach.

Parkinson’s Disease Progression Prediction

healthcare modelling - Regression

Modelled the progression of Parkinson’s disease using patient-level features. Compared regression approaches and evaluated predictions through clinically relevant error metrics. The work highlights the challenges and opportunities in modelling longitudinal health data.

Forecasting Household Electricity Consumption

patterns over time - Time Series

Analysed time series to uncover structure, trends, and seasonality, then built and validated forecasting models using classical ARIMA/SARIMA frameworks. Diagnostics and error measures were used to ensure robustness.

Classifying Credit Card Discussion Comments

understanding text - NLP

Clustered customer discussion on Reddit on subreddit Credit Card into categories. Built classification model to allot new comments into one of the clusters. This helped summarize customer concerns and identify dominant themes useful for improving service offerings.
